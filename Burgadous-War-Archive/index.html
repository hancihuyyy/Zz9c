Executive Summary Notice BJ-431

On First Light, Sunfall 14 of the Verdant Month, Year 241 of the Obsidian Throne, Sect J fielded roughly 4,000 infantry and light cavalry against a Burgadous Alliance force twice its size. For ten sunfalls, steel met steel on the eastern plain. The turning point arrived when Commander Telrik—Sect J’s logistics chief—defected and exposed the supply routes. With depots burned and wells seized, Burgadous Ladous Nadous fell after an **eleven-day siege**. The sect’s authority collapsed.

Finding: The defeat was driven by logistics failure and political betrayal, not sorcery or machines. Pikes, shields, terrain, and timing decided the day. The weight of an army is measured in its stomach, not its swords.

Incident Timeline

Sunfall 1–3 Sect J advances; Alliance pushed back 7 li from the walls.

Notes: Field skirmishes favor Sect J, but the rain-slicked fields make movement slow and treacherous. The men chant as they march, but the sound is swallowed by the endless downpour. Mud clings to their boots, heavy and cold, a constant reminder of the difficult path ahead.

Sunfall 4–6 Heavy rain ruins powder; fighting shifts to spears, bows, and shields.

Notes: Soldiers are exhausted, their bronze swords brittle from repeated clashes. The mud is thick, making the simple act of lifting a shield a chore. Whispers of desertion begin to spread through the ranks, fueled by hunger and despair.

Sunfall 7 Night raid succeeds; Sect J captures outer granary but cannot hold it.

Notes: The brief taste of victory is hollow; grain is a fleeting luxury, and the true prize remains behind the walls. We fight with the ghosts of a full stomach, a cruel phantom of a meal we will never have.

Sunfall 8 Telrik defects; mule road and depots torched; water points seized.

Notes: The smoke from the North Road depot can be seen from the city walls. This is the moment morale collapses. The men are not just hungry, they are betrayed. Their trust in Telrik, a man who shared their rations, turns to bitter ash.

Sunfall 9 Final message from Telrik received, offering terms. General Corveth refuses.

Notes: The message from Telrik is a polished piece of parchment, a stark contrast to our torn and muddy gear. Corveth's refusal is met with a weary silence. The men know they are being led to their deaths, but their loyalty holds firm, a final act of defiance.

Sunfall 10 Corveth orders wedge assault at the East Ditch; half the force lost.

Notes: The final charge is a desperate, bloody gamble. The ditch becomes a killing ground, filled with broken pikes and the cries of the starving. Corveth dies with his face to the sky, a hero to some, a fool to many.

Sunfall 11 Inner gates opened at dusk; brief street combat; Sect J surrenders.

Notes: The final hours are a blur of surrender and exhaustion. The citizens mourn their dead, and the soldiers of Sect J lay down their arms, no longer fighters, just men.

Analysis — Why Sect J Lost

Logistics: With depots burned and wells seized on Sunfall 8, Sect J lost endurance. Short rations crippled the night watch and the reserve lines before the decisive assault. The hunger was a weapon more effective than any blade.

Formation Risk: The Sunfall 10 wedge attack created momentum but overexposed the flanks. Once the tip stalled at the ditch, the formation became a killing ground. The bronze swords were no match for the Alliance's iron-tipped spears, and the mud turned a tactical mistake into a massacre.

Doctrine: ARKH-5GC
Guard supply lines with mixed companies; never concentrate command and logistics in one officer; rotate gate wardens at dusk. Status: Adopted by Alliance garrisons.

Risk Markers: empty wells near city lines; smoke columns at mule roads; units trading shields for speed.

Aftermath & Community Impact

  • Sect J: Disbanded; remaining companies absorbed into Alliance militia. Their banner was burned in a public square, a final, shameful act.
  • Telrik: Elevated to council; wrote grain-law favoring frontier towns. He is both a hero and a villain in popular folklore, a man who saved his people by betraying his cause.
  • Economy: Markets reopened in 30 sunfalls; salt convoys rerouted via South Gate. The city still faces a labor shortage from the casualties of war.
  • Civilians: Bell toll each Arkh-11 at dusk to honor the fallen. A ritual of remembrance where the people whisper the names of the dead into the wind, believing it carries their souls to the heavens.
Officer’s Note (Marek): “Steel won the gate; bread kept it.”

Urban Myths Still Circulating

  • At dusk on Arkh-11, the East Ditch “rings” if you set a broken sword on its bank.
  • The North Road still smells of wet grain after rain, no barns in sight.
  • A ledger somewhere lists Telrik under both “Savior” and “Traitor.”

FAQ

Was Commander Telrik solely responsible?

No. His defection exposed the supply web, but weather and risky formations amplified the damage. The truth is, the army was already starving and on its last legs when he made his move.

Why wasn’t a negotiated surrender accepted earlier?

Sect J demanded control of the inner markets; the Alliance refused. Talks collapsed on Sunfall 6. General Corveth was a stubborn man, and his pride was a greater enemy than the Alliance's army.

What happened to Captain Renna's force?

Captain Renna and her force successfully covered the retreat of the main army, but became separated from them in the process. With their numbers decimated, they were eventually absorbed into the local militia and scattered across the region. Rumor has it that Renna herself now lives as a hermit in the wilderness.

Are there any survivors of the battle?

While most of Sect J’s soldiers were either killed or absorbed into the Alliance, many non-combatant survivors of the siege live on. Their accounts, passed down from generation to generation, remain a crucial part of the city's oral history.
